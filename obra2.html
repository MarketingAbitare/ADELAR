CUERDAS BATIDAS. BEATEN ROPES

Medium: Pigments, aluminium, mirrors, nylon

Dimensions: 40 X 60X 5 cm

Year: 2025

Hanging Hardware: Included (ready to hang)

This piece is part of a contemporary kinetic art series that explores the intersection of light, movement, and perception. Using materials like reflective aluminum, mirrored surfaces, transparent nylon threads, and vibrant pigments, the work creates a dynamic visual field that changes depending on the viewer’s position and the surrounding light. The surface may appear static at first glance, but subtle shifts in angle reveal an ever-changing play of reflections, shadows, and optical vibrations. The nylon elements gently react to ambient air currents, adding a quiet, almost imperceptible movement to the piece — a reminder that stillness is never absolute. The geometric composition is precise, yet it opens space for emotion and contemplation. Mirrors fragment and multiply the environment, inviting the viewer to become part of the work itself. The pigments are carefully chosen and layered to evoke energy, depth, and balance. This series was born from a desire to reconnect with the essential: light, form, time, and the presence of the observer. Influenced by the kinetic and optical art traditions of Latin America and Europe, the piece pays tribute to the pioneers of the movement while pushing the dialogue forward with new materials and personal vision. Crafted with archival-quality materials and professional techniques, the work is durable, easy to maintain, and designed for long-term display. It comes with discreet hanging hardware already installed.
